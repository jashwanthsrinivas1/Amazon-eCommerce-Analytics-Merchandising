# Project 01 — WebLab A/B Testing: AC Category Landing Page

**Type:** Controlled Experimentation & Conversion Analytics
**Category:** Air Conditioners (AC)
**Marketplace:** Amazon.in — Mobile & Desktop
**Role:** Senior Site Merchandiser (experiment owner and analyst)
**Period:** August 2022 – September 2024

---

## Business Problem

Amazon India's Air Conditioner Category Landing Page (CLP) is one of the highest-traffic pages in the Large Appliances category, especially during peak seasons like summer and Great Indian Festival. The page serves millions of customers but converting that traffic into purchases is a constant challenge.

The core problem: **the page was over-indexed on design banners** — large promotional images for brands and affordability offers. While visually rich, this layout required customers to click through multiple pages before seeing actual products. Every additional click is a drop-off risk.

**Key questions the team needed to answer:**
- Does showing products upfront (via ASIN shovelers) reduce customer friction?
- Which page layout — banners only vs. mixed banners and shovelers — drives higher Ordered Product Sales (OPS) and Glance Views (GVs)?
- Which slot positions, creative types, and ASIN selections perform best?

## Experiment Design

### Primary Experiment — AC CLP Mobile Layout Test

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Platform | Mobile (Amazon.in app) |
| Control (C) | All design banners — existing layout |
| Treatment (T1) | Mixed layout — ASIN shovelers + design banners |
| Duration | 15 days (April 10–24, 2023) |
| Primary Metrics | OPS (Ordered Product Sales), Units |
| Secondary Metrics | Glance Views (GVs), Click-Through Rate (CTR) |
| Tool | Amazon WebLab (internal A/B testing platform) |

**Core hypothesis:** Showing ASINs upfront in shoveler format allows customers to reach the product detail page in fewer clicks, reducing friction and increasing purchase intent.

**What changed in T1:**
- Replaced full-banner slots with product shovelers showing real ASINs with price and rating
- Mixed layout retained some design banners for brand storytelling while leading with product visibility

---

## Results

| Metric | T1 vs. Control Impact |
|---|---|
| OPS (Ordered Product Sales) | **+0.42%** |
| Units | **+0.09%** |
| Glance Views — AC CLP | **+47%** |
| CTR on page | **+25 basis points** |
| Week 16 GVs vs. Week 15 | **+64%** |

### What the Numbers Mean

At Amazon's scale, a **+0.42% OPS lift** on a high-traffic category page represents a meaningful revenue impact. The +47% GV increase confirms that customers were engaging more deeply with the page. The +25 bps CTR improvement validates that the shoveler layout was more clickable than the banner-only control.

## Supporting Work

The WebLab experiment did not happen in isolation. It was supported by:

- **Wireframing:** Designed slot-by-slot page layouts for the AC CLP across App and PC, specifying which slot gets which content type, image dimensions, and copy
- **Deal curation:** Selected and configured specific ASINs for deal shoveler slots — energy-efficient 5-star ACs, brand spotlights (LG, Voltas, Daikin, Samsung), and price-band products
- **Campaign coordination:** Ran concurrent coupon campaigns, bank stripe campaigns, and affordability widget placements (No Cost EMI, Exchange Offer)
- **Metric monitoring:** Pulled weekly slot-level CTR, impressions, and GV data from Sophia to track experiment health

---

## Key Learnings

1. **Product-forward layouts outperform banner-heavy pages** on mobile, where screen real estate is limited
2. **Shovelers reduce navigation depth** — customers tap a product directly instead of clicking through an intermediary page
3. **GV growth is a leading indicator** — the +47% GV lift preceded and predicted the OPS improvement
4. **Mixed layouts balance brand storytelling with product visibility** — the T1 mix was the right balance
